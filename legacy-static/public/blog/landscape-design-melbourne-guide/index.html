Landscape Design Melbourne: A Practical Guide to Planning a Premium Outdoor Space

Landscape design in Melbourne works best when the layout, levels, materials, planting and construction plan are considered together before work starts, especially for high-end residential homes.

A strong residential landscape design starts with how the space needs to work. Before choosing paving or plants, map the daily use of the garden: entry, entertaining, bins, storage, pets, children, privacy, sun, shade and drainage.

High-end Melbourne landscaping is strongest when gardens are treated as complete outdoor rooms rather than isolated features. Think in zones, then make each zone feel connected through level changes, material repetition, proportion and planting structure.

For a Lurra Projects client, the practical first step is simple: define the outcome clearly before rushing into finishes. Identify the biggest pain point in the outdoor area. It might be a muddy lawn, an awkward slope, a tired front entry, a poor entertaining area or planting that has become too hard to maintain. The design should solve that first.

The best outcomes usually combine hard landscaping and soft landscaping. Paths, paving, retaining and edging create the structure. Turf, garden beds, trees, hedging and groundcovers soften the space and make it feel settled.

If you are planning a landscape design in Melbourne, ask for a clear scope before construction begins. A good scope should outline surfaces, levels, access, drainage, planting direction and the order of works. That avoids vague expectations and helps the project move cleanly from idea to finished garden.
